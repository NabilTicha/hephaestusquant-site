Governance

Risk management and structure.

How we manage risk, review research, and make decisions. This page exists so that members (and potential members) know exactly how things work.

Risk

Risk framework

Risk is the primary object of analysis — return is a consequence of getting risk right, not the goal itself. Every strategy evaluation starts with decomposing where the risk comes from: factor exposures, idiosyncratic concentration, regime sensitivity.

We assume models will fail, correlations will break, and regimes will shift without warning. The framework is built around that assumption: hard drawdown limits, exposure caps, and rebalancing rules that adjust with detected regime state.

Standards

How we manage risk

  • Preservation bias Position sizing uses worst-case scenarios, not expected-case. We'd rather be undersized in a rally than oversized in a crash.
  • Regime conditioning Exposure limits and rebalancing frequency are tied to regime state. No single set of parameters across all market environments.
  • Decomposition Risk is monitored at three levels — factor, idiosyncratic, regime — each with independent thresholds and response rules.
  • Failure archive Rejected strategies and failed hypotheses are documented with full methodology. The failure log is a core research resource.
Structure

Organizational rules

Research review

All research goes through internal peer review before it informs any investment decision. The review evaluates methodology and statistical validity. Conviction is not a criterion.

Decision separation

The person who generates a research signal doesn't make the deployment decision. Research and risk management are separate functions with separate evaluation criteria.

Important: Hephaestus Quant does not manage capital collectively. Members invest on their own accounts using their own judgment. The group provides research infrastructure and methodology — not investment advice.
