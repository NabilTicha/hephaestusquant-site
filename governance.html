Risk & Governance

Risk-first methodology.

Risk management is not a constraint on research — it is the foundation. Every decision framework starts with an understanding of what can go wrong.

Philosophy

Risk framework

We treat risk as the primary object of analysis. Return is a consequence of correctly identified and managed risk exposures — not the other way around. Every strategy evaluation begins with risk decomposition.

Models fail. Regimes shift. Correlations break. Our framework assumes these events will happen and builds in structural responses: drawdown guards, exposure limits, and regime-conditional rebalancing rules.

Principles

Risk management standards

Capital preservation bias

Asymmetric loss function. We prefer to miss upside rather than realize avoidable drawdowns. Position sizing reflects worst-case, not expected-case scenarios.

Regime conditioning

Risk parameters are not static. Exposure limits, correlation assumptions, and rebalancing frequency are conditioned on detected market regime. A single parameter set across all environments is a model failure.

Multi-level decomposition

Portfolio risk is decomposed into factor risk, idiosyncratic risk, and regime risk. Each level has independent monitoring and independent response mechanisms.

Negative result documentation

Failed strategies and rejected hypotheses are documented with the same rigor as successful ones. The failure archive is a core research asset.

Structure

Organizational governance

Research review

All research output is subject to internal peer review before it informs any investment decision. Review evaluates methodology, statistical validity, and robustness — not conviction.

Decision separation

The person who generates a research signal does not make the deployment decision. Research and risk management operate as independent functions with separate evaluation criteria.

Transparency

All assumptions, parameters, and model limitations are documented and accessible to every member. There are no proprietary black boxes within the research process.

Individual responsibility

Members invest on their own accounts and make their own allocation decisions. Hephaestus Quant provides research infrastructure and methodology — not investment advice or fund management.
