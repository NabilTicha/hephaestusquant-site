Research

Quantamental research.

We operate at the intersection of quantitative and fundamental analysis — systematic factor modeling conditioned on regime-aware risk management.

Approach

What we do

We do not predict markets. We identify structural relationships, test whether they survive out-of-sample, and discard what does not replicate. Every claim is treated as a hypothesis subject to falsification.

Research output is evaluated on reproducibility, not conviction. Negative results are documented with the same rigor as positive ones. There is no distinction in our process between a failed hypothesis and a successful one — both produce information.

Domains

Focus areas

Factor research

Systematic identification, construction, and evaluation of return drivers across equity, fixed income, and cross-asset universes. We study factor decay, interaction effects, and the conditions under which factors fail.

Regime analysis

Markets are non-stationary. We model volatility regimes, correlation structure breaks, and macro state transitions to condition factor exposure and risk allocation.

Risk decomposition

Understanding where risk comes from matters more than where return comes from. We decompose portfolio risk into factor, idiosyncratic, and regime components.

Standards

Methodology

All research follows a falsification-first protocol:

  1. State a falsifiable hypothesis.
  2. Define rejection criteria before running any test.
  3. Use out-of-sample validation.
  4. Report negative results.
  5. Separate research claims from strategy deployment decisions.
