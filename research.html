Research

Quantamental research.

Systematic factor modeling, conditioned on regime state. We try to figure out what drives returns and — more usefully — when it stops working.

Approach

How we work

We don't predict markets. We look for structural relationships in data, test whether they hold out-of-sample, and discard what doesn't replicate. Most hypotheses don't survive this process, which is the point — we'd rather know something is fragile before committing capital to it.

Negative results get documented with the same rigor as positive ones. A conclusive "this doesn't work" is a research output.

Domains

What we focus on

Factor research

Construction and evaluation of return drivers across equities and cross-asset universes. We spend a lot of time on factor decay and the conditions under which factors fail — that's where most of the useful information is.

Regime analysis

Markets aren't stationary. We model volatility regimes, correlation structure breaks, and macro state transitions using HMMs and rolling state models. This conditions everything else we do.

Risk decomposition

Breaking portfolio risk into factor, idiosyncratic, and regime components. We care more about where risk comes from than where return comes from.

Current research questions

  • Regime · Correlation Cross-asset correlation breakdowns near VIX regime transitions (25→40 range). Using DCC-GARCH on G10 equity indices. Question is whether this is structural or an artifact of the 2008/2020 samples.
  • Crowding Short interest concentration as a crowding proxy for value factor decay. Fitting to post-2015 US large-cap. Trying to separate crowding-driven decay from macro-driven decay.
  • Regime · Classification Minimum observation window for reliable 2-state HMM regime classification on realized vol. Currently testing 60/120/252 day windows. Below 60 days, classification noise seems to dominate.
  • Factor · Liquidity Factor exposure drift under varying liquidity conditions in European equities. Comparing factor loadings in top vs. bottom Amihud illiquidity quintiles, rolling 12-month.
Standards

Methodology

Every research claim goes through the same process:

  1. State a falsifiable hypothesis with explicit rejection criteria.
  2. Define the out-of-sample test before looking at results.
  3. Run the test. Report what happened, including null results.
  4. If it replicates, stress-test against regime subsamples and transaction cost assumptions.
  5. Separate the research conclusion from any deployment decision — those are different processes with different criteria.
